The Race between Man and Machine

Anxiety about automation is prevalent in this era of rapid technological advances especially in artificial intelligence, machine learning, and robotics. However, not all technical advances put occupations at risk. Rather, technology would substitute for jobs who perform some tasks and would complement others. Then, which tasks are more, or less, susceptible to automation in the 21st century, in terms of technical advances and labor substitutivity/complementarity? To answer the fundamental question, we initiate a research project entitled "A Data-Driven Exploration of the Race between Man and Machine." As part of the project, the online system of job automation index provides the task-level susceptibility to automation across occupations and cities in the United States during 2008-2020.
